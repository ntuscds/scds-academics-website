# SCDS Sub-clubs

The Students Computing and Data Science (SCDS) Club hosts several specialized sub-clubs focused on different areas of computing and data science. These sub-clubs provide a platform for students to deepen their knowledge in specific domains, work on specialized projects, and connect with peers who share similar interests.

## About Our Sub-clubs

Each SCDS sub-club operates semi-autonomously with its own leadership team, events, and projects while remaining under the umbrella of the main SCDS organization. This structure allows for focused, in-depth exploration of specific fields while maintaining access to the resources and community of the larger club.

## Our Sub-clubs

### [AI/ML Club](./aiml)

The Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Club focuses on exploring the latest developments in AI and ML technologies, from fundamental concepts to cutting-edge research applications.

**Activities include:**
- Paper reading groups
- Model implementation workshops
- Project collaborations
- AI/ML competitions
- Industry talks and seminars

### [HPC Club](./hpc)

The High-Performance Computing Club concentrates on parallel computing, distributed systems, and computational techniques for solving complex problems that require significant processing power.

**Activities include:**
- Cluster programming workshops
- Optimization techniques
- GPU computing sessions
- HPC facility tours
- Parallel algorithm implementation

### [Cybersecurity Club](./cybersecurity)

The Cybersecurity Club explores various aspects of computer security, including network security, cryptography, ethical hacking, and security policy.

**Activities include:**
- Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions
- Security audit workshops
- Penetration testing practice
- Security tool demonstrations
- Bug bounty programs

## Joining a Sub-club

All SCDS members are welcome to join any of the sub-clubs based on their interests. No prior experience is necessary to join, as each club provides resources and support for members at all skill levels.

To join a sub-club:

1. Attend any of their events (check the SCDS calendar for upcoming activities)
2. Join their dedicated communication channels (Discord/Telegram)
3. Participate in their introductory sessions at the beginning of each semester

## Starting a New Sub-club

If you're passionate about a computing field not currently represented by our existing sub-clubs, we encourage you to propose a new one! The SCDS executive committee reviews proposals for new sub-clubs each semester.

To propose a new sub-club, prepare a brief proposal that includes:

- The focus area and objectives
- Potential activities and events
- Resources required
- Initial leadership team
- Plan for sustainability

Submit your proposal to the SCDS executive committee for review.

## Sub-club Resources

Each sub-club has access to:

- Dedicated meeting spaces
- Equipment relevant to their domain
- Funding for events and projects
- Connections to faculty advisors and industry partners
- Promotion through SCDS channels
